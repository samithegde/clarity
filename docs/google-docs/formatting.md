# Google Docs — Text and Paragraph Formatting

## Change font, size, and color

- **Font**: Click the font name dropdown in the toolbar (default: Arial) and choose a font
- **Size**: Click the font size field and type a value, or use the up/down arrows
- **Color**: Click the **A** with the color bar in the toolbar → pick from the palette or enter a hex code
- **Highlight**: Click the highlight icon (marker icon) → pick a color

## Headings and styles

1. Select the text to format
2. Click the **Styles** dropdown (shows "Normal text" by default)
3. Choose: Normal text, Heading 1, Heading 2, Heading 3, Title, Subtitle

## Line spacing

1. Click **Format** → **Line & paragraph spacing**
2. Choose: Single, 1.15, 1.5, Double — or click **Custom spacing** for exact values

## Indentation

- Click **Format** → **Align & indent** → **Increase indent** or **Decrease indent**
- Or use the ruler at the top: drag the indent markers left or right

## Page setup (margins, orientation, paper size)

1. Click **File** → **Page setup**
2. Change orientation (Portrait / Landscape), paper size, and margins
3. Click **OK** or **Set as default**

## Columns

1. Click **Format** → **Columns**
2. Choose 1, 2, or 3 columns — or click **More options** for custom column widths and spacing

## Headers and footers

1. Click **Insert** → **Headers & footers** → **Header** or **Footer**
2. Type your header or footer text
3. Click outside the header/footer area to close it

## Page numbers

1. Click **Insert** → **Page numbers**
2. Choose whether to show numbers at the top or bottom, and the starting number

## Table of contents

1. Place your cursor where you want the TOC to appear
2. Click **Insert** → **Table of contents**
3. Choose a style (with links or plain text)
